Here's the lineage with meanings substituted (using the most common interpretive set for this famous exercise, cross-referenced with Hitchcock where possible):
Adam → Man (or "Earthy/Red"; from adomah, earth/ground)
Seth → Appointed (or "Put/Fixed"; Hitchcock: "Put; Who Puts; Fixed")
Enos (Enosh) → Mortal (or "Mortal man; sick; despaired of"; Hitchcock: "Mortal Man; Sick; Despaired Of; Forgetful")
Cainan (Kenan) → Sorrow (or "Possessor/Purchaser" in Hitchcock, but root often linked to "dirge/lament/sorrow" in etymological studies)
Mahalalel → The Blessed God (or "Praise of God"; combines mahalal = praise + el = God)
Jared → Shall come down (or "A ruling; commanding; coming down"; Hitchcock: "a ruling; commanding; coming down")
Enoch → Teaching (or "Dedicated/Disciplined"; Hitchcock: "dedicated; disciplined")
Methuselah → His death shall bring (often interpreted as "When he dies, it shall be sent" or "his death shall bring"; Hitchcock variants like "He Has Sent His Death" or "Who Demands His Death")
Lamech → The Despairing (or "Poor; made low"; Hitchcock: "poor; made low")
Noah → Rest (or "Comfort/Consolation"; Hitchcock: "Repose; Consolation")
