Exegetical Analysis of the Bible
================================

## Output

**For each chapter of the Bible:**
Provide a detailed exegetical analysis of the chapter.
Answer each question as a scholar to an amateur audience.
Answers that exceed a page of text should be split into multiple files.

### Structure
/research
  /01_Genesis
    /01_History.md  -- the historical analysis of Genesis chapter 1
    /01_Scientific.md  -- philosophical and technological analysis of Genesis chapter 1

_Caveats_:
* Single chapter books can be split per verse; i.e. do the Research analysis per verse instead of per chapter.
* The bolded element below should always be completed;  the other questions may not be pertinent to the passage.


Research
========

## Category: Context
  How does the chapter fit into it's book?

  What is the **historical context** of the chapter?
  What is the **cultural context** of the chapter?

## Category Language
  What are the **linguistic features** of the chapter?
  * meaning
  * syntax
  * grammar
  What are the **literary features** of the chapter?
  * genre
  * structure
  * devices
  * idioms

## Category: Science
  What, if any, are the philosophical elements of the chapter?
  What, if any, are the psychological elements of the chapter?
  What, if any, are the technological elements in the chapter?
  What, if any, are the information-science/computing aspects of the chapter?

## Category: Pastoral
  What is the **theological significance** of the chapter?
  What spiritual disciplines are in play in the chapter?
  What timeless principles are in view in the chapter?

  What is the **plenary authorship intent** in composing the chapter?
  What, if any, are the human image-bearer elements of the chapter?
  What, if any, are the wisdom encodings of the chapter?
  How does the chapter fit within over-arching canonical threads and themes?

  Clarify the original meaning of the chapter for a modern audience.
  **Application**: what attributes or actions, of the modern audience, must change according to the chapter? and how might we implement these changes?
